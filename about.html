What is Money$ense?

MoneySense is a program in the Puget Sound region that resides within High Schools to teach students about how to manage their own personal finances in the future. This includes how to spend and save wisely, how to keep a budget, how to avoid debt, and all in all, how to create a more financially stable future for our world. We do this by sending volunteers with custom material into classes where they run activities to help students learn the material in a unique and interactive way. We combine this with online resources, including a game and a personal financial manager software that will make this program even more intuitive for the students.

We are a non-profit organization based out of Bothell, WA. At this point, as of November 2013, we are in the early development phases. Two years ago, in October 2011, we won a grant from Ashoka Seattle at SIFP Seattle 2011 (Social Innovation Fast Pitch), now called SVP (Social Venture Partners) Fast Pitch Showdown. Our goal through this event was to get recognition with schools, volunteers, and potential investors. We hoped to attract financial institutions and companies to invest in us, as we are creating future responsible customers for them. We also need volunteers to teach the program in schools, and we need schools that will allow us to teach our material. The purpose of the prototype on this website is to present to these potential contributors the structure with which our ending product will be built.
